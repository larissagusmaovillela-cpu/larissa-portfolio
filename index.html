03
UGC Strategy Video Editing Paid Social Conversion

Scaling UGC
Performance

Challenge

UGC creators delivered long-form content. The raw footage had potential — but one video running as-is wasn't enough.

Strategy

Multiply each asset. One creator video isn't one ad. It's five different ads with five different angles, hooks, and audiences.

Execution

Developed a modular editing system that extracted maximum value from each creator collaboration. Variations were tested systematically, not randomly.

Every creator video was treated as raw creative material rather than a finished advertisement. By testing multiple hooks, messaging angles and edits, a single piece of UGC became several unique campaigns. The winning variation generated more than 200 sales in one week.

200+

Sales generated in a single week by one creative variation — extracted from a single piece of UGC footage through systematic re-editing and hypothesis testing.

How I think.

—

People connect with stories before they connect with products.

Emotion unlocks attention. Attention enables persuasion. Persuasion drives conversion. Start with the human, not the product.

—

Quantity creates quality. You can't think your way to a winning creative.

The best ad isn't discovered in a meeting. It emerges from a relentless cycle of making, testing, and learning. Volume is the prerequisite for breakthroughs.

—

Creative is a system. Systems scale. Taste alone doesn't.

Good creative intuition is valuable. A repeatable system that produces good creative at scale is priceless. Build the process, not just the output.

—

Testing beats opinions. Always. No exceptions.

The market decides what works — not the team, not the founder, not the creative director. The only valid opinion is the one the data confirms.

—

Growth lives in the iteration, not the launch.

The first version is never the best version. The discipline to ship, learn, and improve faster than anyone else is the real competitive advantage.

—

AI should amplify creativity. Not simulate it.

The best use of AI is removing friction from execution so humans can spend more time on what only humans can do: genuine insight, emotional truth, and original thinking.
